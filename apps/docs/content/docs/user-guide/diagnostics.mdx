title: Diagnostics

Templara constantly checks your document and tells you about problems **before** you export. That's **Diagnostics** — think of it as a spell-checker for your document.

## Where to find it

A **diagnostics badge** sits near the bottom of the editor. It gives you an at-a-glance status. Click it to open the **Diagnostics** panel, which lists every issue found.

![The diagnostics badge and open Diagnostics dock showing status and issue groups.](/user-guide/diagnostics-dock.png)

## The three severities

Issues are grouped into three levels so you know what's urgent:

| Level | Meaning | Do you have to fix it? |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Blocking** | A real problem that will break the output. | **Yes** — fix before exporting. |
| **Warnings** | Something that might not look right (e.g. content overflow, a possibly-missing value). | Recommended — review it. |
| **Info** | Helpful notes, not problems. | No — informational only. |

## How to use it

1. Click the badge to open the panel.
2. Start with **Blocking** items — these must be fixed.
3. Read each message; it describes the problem in plain terms.
4. Where possible, the diagnostic points you to the element or field involved so you can jump to it and fix it.
5. Re-check the badge — it updates as you fix things.

## Common things it flags

- **Missing data / binding** — you bound to a field that isn't in the data.
- **Overflow** — content is too big for its space or the page.
- **Repeat layout issues** — a repeating row doesn't fit or paginate cleanly.

## Why this is your friend

It's much better to learn about a problem here than to send a customer a broken invoice. Make a habit of glancing at the badge before every export, and clear all **Blocking** items first.

> We're actively improving how this panel looks and how easily it jumps you to the exact problem. The information it gives you is reliable today; the polish is getting better.
