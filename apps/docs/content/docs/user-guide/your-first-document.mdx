This page walks you through your very first session, click by click. No prior knowledge assumed.

## Step 1 — The dashboard (the first screen you see)

When Templara opens, you land on the **dashboard**. This is your home base.

![The Templara Studio dashboard, showing the Welcome banner, Recent Projects, and the Create from template grid.](/user-guide/dashboard.png)

You'll see three areas:

1. **Welcome banner** — with two buttons: **Open current project** (continue where you left off) and **Start from Invoice** (jump straight into an invoice).
2. **Recent Projects** — cards for documents you've already worked on. Click one to reopen it.
3. **Create from template** — starter layouts you can build on (see Step 2).

There's also a **View Docs** button in the top-right corner, which brings you to this documentation.

## Step 2 — Create a project

A **project** is one document you're working on. You have two ways to start:

**Option A — Start from a ready-made template (recommended for your first time).**
In the "Create from template" grid, click one of these:

| Template | What it is |
| --- | --- |
| **Blank** | An empty page. Total freedom, but you build everything yourself. |
| **Invoice** | Line items, totals, and invoice sample data already set up. |
| **Shipment BOL** | A bill of lading with addresses and codes. |
| **Receipt** | A compact receipt with repeating rows and totals. |
| **Pay Stub** | Payroll layout with earnings and deductions. |
| **Shipping Label** | A small label with an address block, barcode, and QR code. |

Clicking a template creates a **new, separate project** you can edit freely — it won't change the original starter.

**Option B — Start blank.** Choose **Blank** if you want to design from scratch. New to Templara? Start from a template instead so you can see how a finished layout is put together.

> **Tip:** Every starter comes with **sample data** already loaded, so your preview shows realistic content immediately.

## Step 3 — You're now in the editor

After you pick a template, Templara opens the **editor** — the design workspace. It can look busy the first time. Don't worry: the next page, [Tour of the interface](/docs/user-guide/the-interface), labels every part.

![The Templara editor open on the Invoice template, with the tool rail, layers, data panel, canvas, and inspector all visible.](/user-guide/editor-overview.png)

For now, just know:

- The **big white page in the middle** is your document (the canvas).
- The **strip of icons on the far left** is where you pick tools to add things.
- The **panels around the edges** let you manage layers, connect data, and change properties.

## Step 4 — Make a small change

Let's prove it works:

1. Click any piece of text on the page. A blue selection outline appears.
2. Look at the **right-hand panel** (the inspector) — it now shows that element's properties.
3. Try changing something, like the text or its size.

You just edited a template. Nothing is broken — you can always undo with **Ctrl/Cmd + Z**.

## Step 5 — Preview with real data

To see what your document actually looks like when filled in:

1. Find the **Preview** button (bottom-right of the editor).
2. Click it and choose **Preview (sample data)**.
3. A full-screen preview opens showing the finished document — real values, expanded rows, proper pages.
4. Close the preview to return to editing.

This is the "editing vs. previewing" idea from the [overview](/docs/user-guide/overview) in action.

## Step 6 — Save your work

Templara keeps your projects **in your browser** automatically, but you should still save on purpose:

1. Open the **Project** menu in the top toolbar (it shows your document's name).
2. Click **Save project**.

From the same menu you can also **Rename**, **Duplicate**, **Delete**, or **Export** your project as a `.templara.json` file (a backup you can re-import later or share).

> **Note:** Because projects live in your browser, clearing your browser data can remove them. Use **Export** to keep a safe copy of anything important.
