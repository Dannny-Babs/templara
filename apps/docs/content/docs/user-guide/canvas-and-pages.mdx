This page covers the tools that help you **see and align** your document, plus how to work with multiple pages. None of these helpers appear in the final document — they're just for you while designing.

## Zooming and moving around

The **bottom-left dock** controls your view:

- **Zoom in / out** — get closer for detail or step back for the whole page.
- **Zoom level** — type an exact percentage, or pick a preset: **Fit page**, **50%**, **75%**, **100%**, **125%**, **150%**.
- **Fit page** is the quickest way to re-center if you get lost.

![The bottom-left zoom control with presets open.](/user-guide/zoom-dock.png)

## Alignment helpers

These make things line up neatly:

- **Rulers** — measurement bars along the top and left edges.
- **Grid** — a faint background grid to align to.
- **Guides** — lines you can place to align multiple elements.
- **Snapping** — when on, elements "click" into place against the grid and guides so they line up automatically.

You can toggle **rulers**, **snap to grid**, and **snap to guides** from the canvas controls. If elements feel like they're "jumping" as you drag, that's snapping helping you — turn it off if you want completely free placement.

## Margins and bleed

- **Margins** — the safe area inside the page edges. Keep important content inside the margins so nothing gets cut off when printed. You can show margin guides to see this boundary.
- **Bleed** — a small area extending past the page edge, used when a design needs color running right to the edge for printing. There's a bleed overlay you can show while designing.

> **Tip:** margin and bleed overlays are **guides only** — they won't print. If your canvas looks cluttered with extra lines, you can hide the overlays you're not using so you see just the document.

## Working with pages

Some documents have more than one page.

- The **Pages panel** lists your pages and shows how many you have.
- The **page switcher** (bottom-center) lets you jump between pages.
- The editor always shows **one page at a time** — the active page.

> Remember: in the **preview**, long content (like a big list) can flow onto **extra pages automatically**, even if you only designed one. That's the renderer paginating for you — see [Preview & export](/docs/user-guide/preview-and-export).
